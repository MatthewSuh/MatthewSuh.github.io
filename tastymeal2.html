Designing a Website for a Client: A Tasty Meal (맛식)

Background

This is a project that I completed for my class INFO 2300: Intermediate Design and Programming for the Web. For this assignment we were told to find a client who needed a website and design one based on their needs and their target audience's needs. The requirements are listed here:

Base Requirements

  • You must find a client. You will design and build a website for this client.
  • The website should be designed for your client's target audience(s).
  • The website must be written in PHP and be backed by a database.
  • Your project must include an adequate amount of user interaction demonstrating your skills with PHP as well as your creativity.

This project was different from my other projects because it wasn’t something that I had thought of on my own and decided to tackle. Instead, this was a group project and the problem would be given to me. The first and most important step was to find a client and meet with them in order to understand his needs/wants, as well as the specific target audience.

While we all worked together to build the website, equally dividing the work, I spent a little more time with the usability and user experience of the website, while other focused on programming and the visual aesthetic.

The Client

The client that we chose was someone on campus who was trying to create a meal prep service, similar to Blue Apron, but with a focus on Korean cuisine.

Understanding the Client’s Needs:

From our meeting, we gathered the following information regarding our client’s needs/wants:

  • Make a website easy and intuitive to use
  • Attract new customers
  • Display all recipes and their ingredients
  • Meet dietary restrictions
  • Create and log into accounts to see subscription(s), previous orders, preferences, etc.

The Target Audience

  1. New Customers: Ease of use and intuitive aspects of the navigation bar, signup section and the example photos for the dishes will all be key points for this audience group. It is imperative that the website place an emphasis on this audience group when creating the website as it is a startup and the primary focus group.
  2. Existing Customers: This audience group would primarily be interacting with the ordering part of the website. Looking at their past orders, new items to order and the subscription service.

Through user research and interviews, we determined that the two biggest user needs are to be able to clearly see prices and what they are purchasing.
